\begin{abstract}
RAG routers are usually optimized as if their candidate actions already contain a
useful, query-dependent trade-off. That premise is testable before a learned policy
is credited. Using sealed traces, we retrospectively apply an integrated
failure-first audit that separates the best fixed action from a label-aware
per-query oracle. Their difference, \emph{routing headroom}, quantifies whether
selection can improve strict utility at all. The controlled study contains 800
grouped scenarios, eight evidence and memory tasks, five pinned generators, and
17 retained direct and composite-grounding actions. The sealed test contains
2,040 route--query traces. Success jointly
requires answer correctness, exact required-evidence citations, perfect citation
precision, correct abstention, parseability, and no forbidden evidence. We also
measure selected-T4 board energy during generation; retrieval and whole-system
energy lie outside that boundary.

The 11-action resident-eligible pool had a best fixed success of 12.5\% and an oracle
ceiling of 12.5\%: all 15 successes came from one direct route, leaving 0.0
percentage points of routing headroom. By contrast, matched direct/grounded pairs
for three 3B--4B references had 13.3--21.7 points of headroom, and the complete
17-action matrix had 29.2 points. Retrieval was identical across grounded
generators and complete for 80.0\% of evidence-required questions, yet strict
success ranged from 0\% to 58.9\%, localizing much of the variation after evidence
acquisition. A task-aware, non-learned direct/grounded oracle improved both success
and measured generation energy relative to always grounding for the capable
references. The frozen learned controller nevertheless had no validation-feasible
threshold, selected one fail-closed fallback for every test query, achieved 0\%,
and saved no energy. The study therefore identifies action-pool routability as an
empirical prerequisite: calibration cannot manufacture capability or
complementarity absent from the eligible actions.
\end{abstract}
