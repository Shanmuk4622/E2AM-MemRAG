\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Retrieval-Augmented and Adaptive Generation}

RAG combines a parametric generator with retrieved non-parametric evidence
\cite{Lewis2020RAG}. Sparse retrieval remains a strong and interpretable baseline;
our lexical routes implement the BM25 probabilistic relevance formulation
\cite{RobertsonZaragoza2009BM25}. Dense passage retrieval and sentence-level
bi-encoders provide complementary semantic signals
\cite{KarpukhinEtAl2020DPR,ReimersGurevych2019SBERT}. The hybrid routes in this
study fuse sparse and dense ranks and then perform a bounded deterministic second
hop, rather than adding an independently trained reranker.

Always-on retrieval is not universally optimal. FLARE initiates retrieval when a
model encounters uncertain future content \cite{JiangEtAl2023FLARE}; Adaptive-RAG
routes questions among no-retrieval, single-step, and iterative strategies based on
estimated complexity \cite{JeongEtAl2024AdaptiveRAG}; and Self-RAG learns retrieval
and critique tokens to regulate evidence use \cite{AsaiEtAl2024SelfRAG}. These
systems motivate conditional grounding, but they do not establish that a compact
generator receives a positive quality--energy return from a particular evidence
contract. \system{} instead freezes the generator-route combinations and measures
their paired effects before evaluating a router.

\subsection{External Memory and Evidence Quality}

External memory extends the evidence problem from documents to persistent,
time-indexed state. LongMem separates a frozen backbone from a long-term memory
network \cite{WangEtAl2023LongMem}; MemoryBank studies acquisition, retrieval,
updating, and forgetting \cite{ZhongEtAl2024MemoryBank}; and MemGPT frames tiered
memory management as an operating-system problem \cite{PackerEtAl2023MemGPT}.
Our flat, hierarchical, and graph/temporal routes are not new memory architectures.
They are controlled mechanisms for testing whether organization, recency, entity
links, and tombstones change strict grounded success under a fixed context budget.

Evaluation must distinguish answer plausibility from evidential support. KILT
couples knowledge-intensive tasks with provenance \cite{PetroniEtAl2021KILT}, while
ALCE evaluates citation correctness and completeness for generated answers
\cite{GaoEtAl2023ALCE}. In the present benchmark, citations are exact identifiers
in a generated evidence universe. The guard checks parseability, retrieved-set
membership, exact required-evidence recall, precision, and forbidden evidence; it
is deterministic and is not described as semantic entailment.

Imperfect retrieval can introduce stale, conflicting, irrelevant, or adversarial
context \cite{ParkLee2024RobustRALM}, and additional context may be detrimental
even when it appears topically related \cite{OhThorne2023DetrimentalContexts}.
These observations motivate our four corruption conditions and the decision to
report abstention and clean-baseline efficacy alongside robustness deltas.

\subsection{Routing, Calibration, and Abstention}

FrugalGPT showed that cascades can reduce API cost while preserving or improving
task quality \cite{ChenZahariaZou2024FrugalGPT}. Hybrid LLM learns a quality-aware
router between local and remote models \cite{DingEtAl2024HybridLLM}, and RouteLLM
uses preference data to route between stronger and weaker endpoints
\cite{OngEtAl2024RouteLLM}. Recent benchmark evidence further shows that router
rankings depend strongly on the model pool and that sophisticated controllers can
approach simple baselines when the pool is poorly separated
\cite{LiEtAl2026LLMRouterBench}. These works primarily optimize monetary or model
selection cost. Our action is a composite of generator, retrieval, memory, and
verification choices, and the measured cost is physical GPU-board energy during
generation.

The controller combines probability calibration with a reject option. Post-hoc
calibration is essential when probabilities drive operational decisions
\cite{GuoEtAl2017Calibration}, while selective prediction formalizes the trade-off
between coverage and risk \cite{GeifmanElYaniv2019SelectiveNet}. We therefore report
calibration, execution coverage, and abstention separately. A low Brier score at
near-zero confidence is not interpreted as useful discrimination when all selected
test outcomes fail.

\subsection{Energy Reporting for Machine Learning}

Green AI argues that efficiency should be a first-class evaluation dimension
\cite{SchwartzEtAl2020GreenAI}; related work documents the environmental and policy
importance of computationally intensive NLP \cite{StrubellEtAl2019EnergyNLP} and
calls for systematic energy and carbon reporting boundaries
\cite{HendersonEtAl2020EnergyReporting}. Deployment-time inference is itself a
substantial and workload-dependent energy source
\cite{LuccioniEtAl2024PowerHungry}. Zeus demonstrates energy--time Pareto reasoning
for GPU workloads \cite{YouEtAl2023Zeus}, although its primary setting is training.

Our measurement uses NVIDIA Management Library board-power queries
\cite{NVIDIA2026NVML} and trapezoidal integration. The quantity does not include
the host, CPU retrieval, storage, network, or cooling, and it is not converted to
carbon. This narrower claim improves reproducibility but also limits what the
energy results can establish.
