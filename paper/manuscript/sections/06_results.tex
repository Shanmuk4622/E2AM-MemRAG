\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{RQ1: The Resident-Eligible Pool Has Zero Success Headroom}

\input{tables/routability_pools}

The post-hoc audit in Table~\ref{tab:routability-pools} separates the opportunity
present in the action pool from the learned policy. In the 11-action
resident-eligible pool, A00 succeeded on 15 of 120 queries (12.5\%). No other eligible route succeeded
on any query, so the union of successes was exactly the A00 success set. Therefore
$B(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{dep}})=O(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{dep}})=0.125$ and
$H(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{dep}})=0$. This is an empirical impossibility certificate
for strict-success improvement on the sealed matrix: no alternative selector,
including a label-aware one, could beat the fixed A00 route with the same actions.

A00 also had the smallest observed mean generation energy in the eligible pool,
54.71 J/query; all ten zero-success alternatives had higher route means. Thus no
fixed route improved the observed mean-quality/mean-energy point. A label-aware
per-query cost bound is slightly different: while retaining all 15 A00 successes,
it chooses A03 for one A00-failure query and lowers the mean by 0.049 J/query
(0.089\%). The changed trace is same-board. This negligible but nonzero saving is
why zero \emph{success} headroom is not described as a proof of zero cost
headroom. Other cross-board comparisons remain descriptive.

The reference actions show that zero headroom was not a property of the testbed.
The Granite-4.1-3B direct/grounded pair had 20.8 \pp{} of headroom, the SmolLM3 pair
21.7 points, and the Qwen3-4B pair 13.3 points. Across the six offline reference
actions, the oracle reached 72.5\% versus a 44.2\% best fixed route. The complete
17-action matrix reached 73.3\%, leaving 29.2 points of headroom. Complementarity
existed, but the capable complementary actions were unavailable to the controller.
Figure~\ref{fig:headroom} makes this action-set dependence visible: the same test
queries have no utility gap under resident eligibility and a substantial gap once
the sequential reference endpoints are admitted.

\subsection{RQ2: Retrieval Success Does Not Imply Evidence Use}

\input{tables/retrieval_utilization}

All five grounded endpoints received identical ordered knowledge and memory IDs on
all 120 queries. On the 90 evidence-required items, those lists contained every
required ID for 72 queries (80.0\%); the two-hop class was complete for 14 of 15.
This common acquisition layer provides a controlled basis for comparing what
happened after retrieval.

Table~\ref{tab:retrieval-utilization} reveals a large retrieval-to-utilization gap.
The Qwen3-0.6B grounded endpoint parsed only 32.2\% of evidence-task outputs and
never produced a complete citation set or a valid answer. The Granite-4.0-1B
endpoint parsed none. In contrast, Granite-4.1-3B parsed 95.6\%, cited all required
evidence on 77.8\%, and achieved 58.9\% strict success. SmolLM3 parsed every output
but cited all required IDs on only 44.4\%, yielding 43.3\% strict success. Qwen3-4B
reached 56.7\%. Because retrieved IDs were held constant, differences in those IDs
cannot explain the between-generator variation. Formatting, answer formation,
citation transfer, and abstention remain observable downstream failure loci; the
traces do not provide a variance decomposition among them.

\subsection{RQ3: Grounding Is a Generator--Task Interaction}

\begin{figure*}[t]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.97\textwidth]{task_grounding_heatmap.png}
    \caption{Grounded-minus-direct strict success by generator and task class. Each
    cell contains 15 paired queries. Several composite-grounded endpoints lose
    direct-task successes, while only the capable references gain strict successes
    on evidence tasks. The Granite-4.0-1B row is an integration failure.}
    \label{fig:task-heatmap}
\end{figure*}

Across the equal eight-task mixture, composite grounding changed strict success by
$-12.5$ \pp{} for Qwen3-0.6B (95\% interval $[-18.3,-6.7]$), $+23.3$ for
Granite-4.1-3B ($[10.0,37.5]$), $+10.8$ for SmolLM3 ($[-2.5,24.2]$), and $+29.2$
for Qwen3-4B ($[16.7,41.7]$). The 1B Granite endpoints both scored zero and are not
interpreted as a grounding effect. Exploratory difference-in-differences contrasts
make the interaction explicit: the Granite-4.1-3B, SmolLM3, and Qwen3-4B effects
exceeded the Qwen3-0.6B effect by 35.8 $[23.3,48.3]$, 23.3 $[11.7,34.2]$, and
41.7 $[29.2,54.2]$ points, respectively. Granite-4.1-3B also exceeded SmolLM3 by
12.5 $[5.8,20.0]$ points. These post-hoc intervals are not multiplicity-adjusted.

Figure~\ref{fig:task-heatmap} shows why an aggregate ``RAG helps'' statement is
misleading. Qwen3-0.6B already had zero success on every evidence-required class;
the composite endpoint did not make those answers worse. It lost the 15
direct-copy successes without adding an evidence-task success. The capable
references display the opposite pattern: direct routes cover evidence-free
behavior, whereas composite-grounded routes add knowledge, memory, temporal, and
authority successes. Two-hop strict
success remains weak despite 93.3\% retrieval completeness, further separating
evidence acquisition from use.

\input{tables/task_aware_oracle}

The same complementarity yields a constructive post-hoc result. A task-aware rule
that chooses direct operation for no-retrieval and deleted/missing items and the
matched grounded route for the other six classes exactly matches the pair oracle in
this matrix. For Granite-4.1-3B, SmolLM3, and Qwen3-4B, it reaches 65.0\%, 55.8\%,
and 55.8\% success, improving on always grounding by 20.8, 21.7, and 13.3 points.
It also lowers observed generation energy by 14.71, 13.05, and 30.32 J/query,
respectively. The rule receives benchmark task labels and is not deployable as-is;
it demonstrates a utility pattern that a suitable eligible pool and
retrieval-need signal could potentially exploit in a prospective study.

\subsection{Grounded Prompts Increase Measured Generation Work}

Every grounded endpoint had a higher mean selected-board generation energy than its
direct counterpart. The four same-board increases were 146.72 J/query for
Qwen3-0.6B, 119.53 for Granite-4.1-3B, 41.52 for SmolLM3, and 171.71 for Qwen3-4B.
The Granite-4.0-1B increase of 3.88 J is cross-board and descriptive. Grounding also
added 499--632 mean input tokens and, for the four interpretable pairs, 8--38 output
tokens. Per-query energy differences were closely associated with generation-time
differences ($r=0.941$--1.000). These are descriptive associations from route-major
single runs, not a causal decomposition or an intrinsic model-efficiency ranking.

The apparent tension between higher always-grounded energy and the savings in
Table~\ref{tab:task-oracle} is important. The composite grounded action costs more
when invoked, but avoiding it on the two evidence-free classes reduces average
energy while preserving complementary successes. Conditional invocation, not
always-on grounding, creates the useful quality--cost structure.

\subsection{RQ4: The Frozen Controller Confirms the Audit Failure}

\input{tables/confirmatory_result}

No validation threshold met the frozen success, coverage, and abstention
requirements. Under the disclosed completion amendment, the controller selected
A03 for all 120 test queries. It achieved 0\% strict success, used 143.16 J/query,
and had 15\% abstention with 100\% execution coverage. The fixed baseline was the
same A03 trace for every query. Consequently, $\Delta_S=\Delta_E=0$ by identity,
with degenerate intervals. The formal non-inferiority margin passed but is
non-informative at zero utility; the energy-reduction gate failed, and the joint
confirmatory hypothesis failed.

This outcome is not evidence that A03 was the best action for every query. It is
fallback collapse: no predicted action cleared the threshold. The post-hoc oracle
adds the stronger diagnosis that even replacing the controller with a perfect
strict-success selector could not have improved over A00 within the eligible pool.
The learned policy additionally chose the wrong fixed action for the observed test
matrix, but action-pool redesign is required before classifier refinement can create
routing headroom.

\subsection{Compatibility and Corruption Diagnostics}

The retained Granite-4.0-1B actions A04, A12, and A13 never parsed under the frozen
prompt/chat-template/runtime combination and repeatedly reached the 80-token limit
with a degenerate output. We classify this as a model--prompt--runtime compatibility
failure, not evidence that the checkpoint family is generally incapable of RAG.
Repairing the interface after test access would have changed the specification.

Under all four corruptions, the selected policy again used A03 and remained at zero
strict success. A zero degradation from a zero clean reference is a floor effect,
not robust competence. A00 remained at 12.5\% because it ignores evidence, while
A13 remained at zero because of the compatibility failure. The synthetic
compromise marker was never emitted, but that fact does not establish general
prompt-injection resistance.
